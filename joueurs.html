⚔ Combat Actions

Attack Action
Make one or more weapon/unarmed attack rolls. Extra Attack grants more.
Cast a Spell ActionBonusReaction
Cast a spell with a casting time of Action, Bonus Action, or Reaction.
Dash Action
Gain extra movement equal to your Speed this turn (doubled if already dashing).
Disengage Action
Your movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks for the rest of the turn.
Dodge Action
Until your next turn: attackers have disadvantage, you have advantage on DEX saves. Ends if Incapacitated or Speed = 0.
Grapple Action
Attack roll vs. STR/DEX (Acrobatics or Athletics) check. On hit: target is Grappled (Speed 0). You must have a free hand.
Help Action
Give one creature advantage on its next ability check, or on its next attack roll against a target within 5 ft of you.
Hide Action
Stealth (DEX) check vs. passive Perception. Must be heavily obscured or behind cover. Become Invisible to those who fail.
Influence Action
Make a Deception, Intimidation, Performance, or Persuasion check to alter a creature's attitude.
Ready Action
Choose a trigger and an action/move. Use your Reaction to execute it when the trigger occurs before your next turn. Holding a spell requires Concentration.
Search Action
Make a Perception or Investigation check to find something hidden.
Shove Action
Attack roll vs. STR/DEX check. On hit: push 5 ft away or knock Prone.
Study Action
Make an Arcana, History, Insight, Medicine, Nature, or Religion check to recall info or assess a creature's capabilities.
Utilize Action
Activate a magic item or interact with an object beyond a simple free interaction.
Opportunity Attack Reaction
Triggered when a creature you can see leaves your reach without Disengaging. Make one melee attack against it.
Free Object Interaction Free
Draw/sheathe a weapon, open a door, pick up an item, etc. One free per turn; second costs an Action.

🩻 Conditions

Blinded
Can't see. Attacks against you have advantage; your attacks have disadvantage. Auto-fail sight-based checks.
Charmed
Can't attack or target the charmer with harmful abilities. Charmer has advantage on social checks against you.
Deafened
Can't hear. Auto-fail hearing-based checks. Verbal spell components still work.
Exhaustion (levels 1–6)
Each level: −1 to all d20 Tests (cumulative). Level 5: Speed halved. Level 6: death. Removed 1 level per Long Rest.
Frightened
Disadvantage on ability checks and attacks while source of fear is in line of sight. Can't willingly move closer to the source.
Grappled
Speed becomes 0. Ends if the grappler is Incapacitated or if you escape (STR/DEX vs. grappler's Athletics).
Incapacitated
Can't take Actions or Reactions.
Invisible
Can't be seen without special senses. Your attacks have advantage; attacks against you have disadvantage. Still detectable by sound, tracks, etc.
Paralyzed
Incapacitated, can't move or speak. Auto-fail STR and DEX saves. Attacks against you have advantage. Hits within 5 ft are critical hits.
Petrified
Turned to stone. Incapacitated, can't move or speak. Resistance to all damage. Immune to poison and disease. Auto-fail STR and DEX saves.
Poisoned
Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.
Prone
Can only crawl (costs double movement) or stand up (costs half Speed). Melee attacks against you have advantage; ranged attacks have disadvantage. Your attacks have disadvantage.
Restrained
Speed becomes 0. Attacks against you have advantage; your attacks have disadvantage. Disadvantage on DEX saves.
Stunned
Incapacitated, can't move, can only speak falteringly. Auto-fail STR and DEX saves. Attacks against you have advantage.
Unconscious
Incapacitated, can't move or speak. Unaware of surroundings. Prone. Auto-fail STR and DEX saves. Attacks have advantage. Hits within 5 ft are critical hits.
Concentration
Taking damage: CON save (DC = 10 or half damage, whichever is higher). Failed save, being Incapacitated, or casting another Concentration spell breaks it.
